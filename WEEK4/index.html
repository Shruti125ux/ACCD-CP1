LOW-POLY OUTDOOR LIVING SPACE

Exploring Light, Shadow, and Spatial Intimacy Through Minimal Geometry

Software: Blender | Technique: Low-Polygon Modeling

Project Type: 3D Scene Design | Designer: Shruti Amritkar

Project Overview

As an architect trained in spatial planning and currently exploring interaction design, I've always been fascinated by how light transforms space and how shadow defines form. This project represents my exploration of low-polygon 3D modeling techniques in Blender—creating an intimate outdoor living space where geometry, light, and architectural intent converge.

The scene imagines a contemporary pergola structure with integrated seating and dining areas, designed as a contemplative outdoor retreat. Through the deliberate use of low-polygon modeling, I sought to capture the essence of architectural form while exploring how minimal geometry can still convey spatial quality, materiality, and atmospheric depth.

Ideation & Concept Development

Design Intent

The conceptual foundation emerged from my architectural background—specifically, my interest in how outdoor structures mediate between built and natural environments. I envisioned a space that feels both sheltered and open, intimate yet expansive, where the pergola's rhythmic slat system creates dynamic patterns of light and shadow throughout the day.

Spatial Planning

Drawing from my experience in architectural spatial planning, I organized the scene around three functional zones:

  • Primary Outdoor Table: A central table with seating beneath the pergola's protective canopy, positioned to maximize the interplay of filtered light
  • Lounge Seating Zone: Low-profile sofas arranged for conversation, creating a secondary gathering space within the structure
  • Pergola: Thoughtfully proportioned pergola to keep the outdoor space enclosed yet semi-open

Light as Design Element

From my earliest architectural studies, I've been captivated by how light defines space. The pergola's slatted roof isn't merely structural—it's a light modulation device, creating rhythmic bands of illumination and shadow that animate the space below. This interplay between solid and void, light and dark, transforms static geometry into a dynamic spatial experience that changes with the sun's movement.

Learning Process & Approach

Embracing New Software

Coming from architectural software like AutoCAD and SketchUp, Blender initially felt overwhelming—its interface dense with options, its logic different from the parametric modeling I knew.

I approached Blender with the same methodology I apply to architectural design: understand the fundamentals, practice deliberately, iterate constantly. The low-polygon constraint actually helped—it forced me to think about essential form rather than getting lost in details, much like early architectural sketching focuses on massing before refinement.
